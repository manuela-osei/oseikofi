OSEI

an akan name with the meaning "noble or honorable".

This is a story of a man called Kofi Osei. Kofi is from Ghana, Ashanti region to be precise. A ghanaian, who's passion was to become a successfull businessman. He started doing business from the age of 6 and has been doing business till now. The story below is his life story and all that he went through to becoming a successful businessman.

The birth of Yaa Nimo's Son

In January 1962, in a village in the Ashanti region called Ntotroso , a married woman called Yaa Nimo bore a newborn baby. In the old days in the Ashanti Region, children were not delivered in hospitals but at home by midwives. In the case of Yaa Nimo’s baby, there was a midwife but the uncle of the baby, who is the father's brother rushed into the room to help deliver the baby. He cut his umbilical cord and wash him. He did that with the reason to protect the baby from any bad ancestral spirit that anyone could have impacted on the baby an to also bless the baby. The baby was named Kofi Osei. Osei is the family name that he inherited from the dad. In Ghana, names are given based on the day the child was born. They believe that a child born on a particular day has certain exclusive traits, which should be reflected in their names. Therefore, the name Kofi is a day given name for a male child born on a Friday.

Growing up as a child

Kofi Osei lived with the mum and siblings in Kumasi, in a small town called Adako Gyakye. His dad was a farmer, so he lived close to his farm with his other family in the village where he owned a piece of land. Christmas was always celebrated at the dad's place. His mum, Yaa Nimo struggled to raise eleven children on her own, she lost three children which made them eight and worked hard to take care of the eight. She was unable to cater for her children’s schooling, so they all had to work to assist their mother in paying school fees and others. At the age of six, Kofi started to work and save money to support his mother and to help him start school. The mum gave him some money to buy some kerosene, he divided the kerosine into bottles and went out to sell them. On Christmas vacations, when they are in the village with their dad, they gather kola nut from the farm and sell them. Fortunately, he one day found a large amount of money whiles taking a bath at the riveside, which was used to help him start school.

School days

During school days, he was very good at agriculture because he had learnt from his father who was a farmer. He was awarded for his excellent study and practice in agriculture. Every evening after school, he continued with his little business to support the home. He went from selling kerosine to learning to make farm baskets to sell them, which included going to the farm to cut down branches. He learnt to climb coconut trees. One day after a school football match, he and his friends went to the bush and he was asked to get some coconut from the tree. Unfortunately for him this time, he fell from on top of the tree and ended up in the hospital for three months. When he got well, he stopped making the farm baskets to doing shoe shine, which entails the polishing of peoples shoes. He then proceeded to sell toffees, gum, and biscuits at important places like the airport and the stadium. He also helped to clean the stadium with his friends and earned a bit. They allowed him to stay to watch football matches for free. The father’s side of the family knew he was familiar with the city and wouldn’t get missing, so to help generate money for the family they sent him to take his auntie’s husband who was blind to beg for money in the city.

Business Minded

In 1979, he finished Junior Secondary school (middle school) and did not have money to continue schooling. There was a new post office been built in their town and the town leaders wanted him to be a postman which he disliked so he left town to live with a close grandma he knew. She took him as a manager in her Hotel called Nanka memorial hotel in Amakuom Kumasi. With this job, he made enough money to start his own business. He took his first trip to Togo with his friend Kwame to pick up items and brought them to Ghana, Kumasi market to sell. From Togo, he picked items from Benin and from Nigeria. His business expanded and at the age of 25, he bought a car ( Datsun E10) and used it as a taxi. He hired someone to drive the taxi and make the money for him whiles he continued with his business. After making some money he decided to enlarge his business by getting good quality products. He sold his car, having in mind to travel abroad to pick up quality products to sell and help enlarge his business. He first invested his money in a business which went really bad and he had a loss.

The long trip

Back home, he had a very successful older brother and sister but he did not want to depend on their success, so he strived for his own success. July 1987, along with other six friends of his they decided to take the risk and travel to Libya by foot, which took them about two months. They took an STC state bus to Burkina Faso. From there, they continue to Mali with a jeep with was known to be a connection car. From Mali to Algeria at their first border town called Timiaouine with a recruiting jeep. From Timiaouine with another jeep they went to Tamanrasset and from there they took a car called Belet (a car that transport date palm) to Djanet. At Djanet with the dessert leaders, they went by foot for three days to cross the desert. Every morning they left early and took a stop late in the evening.They climbed the Hugo mountain and had their rest in caves at night. They picked weeds along the way to help make fire around them at night to keep away wild animals. At this point in time, they were about one hundred and twenty people crossing the desert to Ghat, Libya. Back to Business

In November 1988, Kofi arrived in Trapani in Italy. Trapani is a city in western Sicily. At Trapani, he got received by an Italian family that gave him a job as a house helper for about two months and he moved Palermo by train in January 1989. In Palermo, we worked as a farmer, then at a construction. In 1991, he moved to Brescia, which is to the north of Italy. At Brescia, he worked at a company that melts metals, then he left to work at a plastics company called Impera, then he left to work at a company called Giordano where they make keys. He also worked at Gidini another key company and he was able to save a gather lots of money to restart his business. He started with second-hand car tyres and fridges, bought from Italy and sent to Ghana, sold in a store that he had bought in the market. He continued working and doing his business that he loved a lot. In 1993 he got married and continued the business with his wife. Kofi used to be a consistent customer of a white man called Victorio Minelli, who sold a lot of second-hand items to people. He really liked Kofi, his dedication, hardworking, seriousness and progress that he impacted in his business. Victorio, therefore, decided to be a business partner with Kofi and invested a large amount of money to his business. He also introduced him to good companies that made brushes and keys and they worked with those companies. They shipped items every month to Ghana and sold them all. The business grew yearly and the KOFICHARI company become number one cleaning brushes and keys supplier in Ghana. The company expanded by starting to make it own brushes and the CEO, Kofi Osei decided to bless others with his success. He has contributed to the lives of a lot of people and children in Ghana who struggle with life as a whole.
